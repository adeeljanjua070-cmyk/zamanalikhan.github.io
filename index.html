★ Overview

Honorary Captain Zaman Ali Khan, Sardar Bahadur (OBI 1st & 2nd Class, IOM, RVM) was one of the most highly decorated Native Officers of the British Indian Army. Over nearly 29 years (29 May 1877 – 15 April 1906), he served on three continents and participated in seven major military campaigns.

He was one of the rarest officers of his era to hold both classes of the Order of British India, the Indian Order of Merit for gallantry, and the Royal Victorian Medal — an extraordinary triple distinction.

"One of the rarest JCO officers to receive both classes of the OBI, the IOM, and the RVM."

⚔️ Military Career

PeriodRegimentNotes
1877–19015th Punjab Infantry (later 5/13th Frontier Force Rifles)Began his career
1 Mar 1901–190640th Pathans (later 5/14th Punjab Regiment)Subedar-Major

The 40th Pathans were an elite Pathan infantry regiment raised in 1858. After 1922 they became 5th Battalion (Pathans), 14th Punjab Regiment. Today they are part of the Punjab Regiment, Pakistan Army (16 Punjab).

Upon retirement on 15 April 1906, he was granted the rank of Honorary Captain — the highest honour for a native officer.

🏛️ Legacy

Honorary Captain Zaman Ali Khan represents a remarkable chapter in British Indian military history — a Pathan officer from the Punjab who rose to the highest honours for native soldiers, serving across three continents.

His descendants continue to preserve his military legacy, service records, medals, and portrait.
